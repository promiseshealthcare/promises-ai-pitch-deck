01
A Structural Psychiatrist Shortage
China has approximately 4 psychiatrists per 100,000 people — roughly one-quarter to one-twelfth the per-capita ratios of Western nations. In Shanghai's premium private sector, the shortage is acute even for patients paying ¥1,500–2,400 per session. Jiahui's 4–5 psychiatrists serve 600,000 annual outpatients. Parkway covers 6 locations with 2–3 named specialists. Demand is real. Supply is structurally constrained.
02
The Between-Session Void
Psychiatrists see patients once every 2–4 weeks. Between sessions, patients have no clinical support. Gaps in structured support are documented as high-risk periods for clinical deterioration. No product at psychiatric grade — with human escalation built in — currently addresses this gap in the premium private patient segment.
03
Digital Tools Without Clinical Governance
Platforms like KnowYourself and Moodpath are digital mental health interventions focused on mood tracking and CBT exercises — without medical-grade clinical oversight. Tools without a human-in-the-loop model are documented to fail at accurately escalating during acute deterioration. Reach without a clinical safety net creates risk, not care.
The Specific Patient Niche We Address
Shanghai's premium private patients — MNC employees, HNW individuals, expatriates — are already paying private clinics ¥1,500–2,400 per session without AI intervention. With Promises Healthcare's expertise in delivering high-end psychiatric care, we are uniquely qualified to solve for this segment.

Risks & Mitigations
Three Mission-Critical
Risks — And How We Address Them
1
AI Fails to Detect Suicidality
The most serious clinical risk in any AI mental health product. A missed high-risk indicator before a crisis event is both a patient safety failure and an existential legal and reputational risk for the venture.
Mitigation
  • 9 months of pre-launch AI safety testing required — safety levels cleared by Promises' doctors before any patient sees the product
  • Escalation management dashboard built with timely alerts and crisis intervention protocols implemented before launch
  • In-person initial consultation and full intake form (with informed consent) required for all patients — flags high-risk cases before handing over to AI
2
Difficulty Finding Qualified Psychiatrists in Shanghai
China has approximately 4 psychiatrists per 100,000 people. Shanghai's premium private sector is a constrained talent market. An inability to hire and retain qualified psychiatrists creates a hard ceiling on clinical capacity and revenue growth.
Mitigation
  • Develop a scalable tele-psychiatry team — not dependent on Shanghai in-person supply alone
  • Hire psychiatrists in Tier 2 cities (Chengdu, Xi'an) for remote tele-consultations — where ¥43K/month is a premium salary
  • Recruit Singapore psychiatrists through the Foreign Physician Short-term Practice Permit pathway — Promises Healthcare's 30-year hiring network is a structural advantage no competitor can replicate
3
Delays in Algorithm Filing & Security Assessment Clearance
CAC algorithm registration must be completed before public launch. Delays in filing or government review could postpone launch, extend the pre-revenue burn period, and create regulatory uncertainty for investors.
Mitigation
  • Establish a mandatory AI ethics committee — internal team plus an external government-backed partner in Shanghai — before filing
  • Engage Tina Liu (AllBright Law Offices) — specialist in medtech regulatory affairs — to manage and advise on all CAC filings from Month 1
  • Begin planning 9 months before intended launch — algorithm filing submitted in Month 4 of pre-launch period, well ahead of launch
